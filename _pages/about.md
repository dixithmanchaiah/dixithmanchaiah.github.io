I am an experimental physicist working on Rydberg atom-based quantum sensing, nonlinear dynamics, and quantum optics. My research focuses on RF electric-field sensing using Rydberg atoms, driven-dissipative atomic systems, synchronization phenomena, and quantum metrology.

My recent work explores nonlinear oscillations and frequency-comb behavior in RF-driven dissipative Rydberg systems, including the role of RF heterodyne driving, interaction-induced feedback, and self-sustained oscillations in thermal vapor cells.

I have also worked on cold-atom thermometry, optical and RF heterodyne methods for field sensing, and single-photon source characterization. More broadly, I am interested in using atomic systems as precision sensors and as controllable platforms for nonlinear and nonequilibrium dynamics.
